The City at a Glance

Meet Bogor

A lush hill city just 60 km south of Jakarta — cooler, greener, and full of character. Famous for its world-class botanical garden, a presidential palace with roaming deer, and streets that smell of rain.

Nestled at the foot of Mount Salak and Mount Gede Pangrango, Bogor is where Jakartans escape when the capital's heat and smog become too much. It's earned the nickname Kota Hujan — the Rain City — for its near-daily afternoon showers, which keep the city blanketed in rich tropical green year-round.

The Dutch built a botanical garden here in 1817 that became one of the finest in Southeast Asia. Next door, the colonial-era Presidential Palace still serves as a working retreat for Indonesia's head of state — you may spot deer grazing on its elegant lawns through the iron fence. Beyond the city center, volcanic peaks, hidden waterfalls, tea plantations, and wildlife parks await.

"Bogor is what happens when a colonial hill station slowly grows into a city but never quite loses its garden soul."

What to See & Do

Places to Explore

From botanical wonders and ancient inscriptions to volcanic hikes and wildlife parks. Tap any card for a local tip.

Must See
Kebun Raya Bogor
The jewel of Bogor — 87 hectares of botanical paradise founded in 1817. Over 3,500 plant species, lotus ponds, ancient rainforest trees, and an orchid house with 3,000+ varieties. You can rent bikes or electric scooters to explore.
🕗 Full day 💰 ~IDR 30,000 📍 City Center
🌿 Come early on weekdays — the garden is serene before 9am. The Grand Garden Café inside offers a dreamy lunch spot overlooking the lily ponds.
Landmark
Istana Bogor (Presidential Palace)
A magnificent white colonial palace sitting within the botanical gardens, originally built in 1744 as a Dutch governor's summer retreat. Now a presidential residence — the 280,000 m² grounds hold 448 artworks and a herd of free-roaming deer.
🕗 1–2 hrs 💰 Free to view 📍 Kebun Raya
🦌 You can't enter the palace itself, but watching the deer through the iron fence from inside the botanical garden is genuinely magical — especially at dawn.
Nature
Taman Safari Indonesia
One of Indonesia's top wildlife attractions, located at Cisarua in the Puncak area (~40 min from Bogor). Drive through open enclosures past lions, tigers, elephants, zebras, and orangutans. An unforgettable experience for all ages.
🕗 Half–full day 💰 ~IDR 200,000+ 📍 Cisarua, Puncak
🦁 Arrive early (8am) to beat Jakarta weekend traffic. Book online for discounts. The evening safari show is worth staying for if you can.
Adventure
Waterfalls (Leuwi Hejo & Cilember)
Bogor's surrounding hills hide dozens of waterfalls. Leuwi Hejo is famous for its turquoise plunge pool; Cilember features seven cascades in a row; Bidadari stands 50m tall. Most require a short jungle hike — worth every step.
🕗 2–4 hrs 💰 IDR 5,000–15,000 📍 Various
👟 Wear waterproof shoes — the jungle paths get muddy fast. Visit midweek to have these gems almost entirely to yourself.
Hiking
Mount Salak & Mount Pangrango
Twin volcanic peaks that frame Bogor's skyline. Mount Salak (2,211m) offers adventurous jungle trails to a sulphur crater. Mount Gede Pangrango National Park is home to rare Javan edelweiss and incredible biodiversity.
🕗 Full day 💰 Permit required 📍 Outskirts
⛰️ Permits for Gede Pangrango must be booked in advance online at bookingpendakian.menlhk.go.id — slots fill quickly on weekends.
History
Batu Tulis (Ancient Inscription)
A sacred inscribed stone from 1533 dedicating it to Sri Baduga Maharaja of the Sunda Kingdom's Pajajaran empire. Located in a small shrine near the garden, it remains an active site of devotion for local visitors.
🕗 30 mins 💰 Free 📍 Bogor Selatan
📜 Open Mon–Sun 8am–4pm (closed Friday). Dress modestly and speak quietly — this is still a place of worship for some.
Museum
Museum Zoologi
A fascinating natural history museum beside the botanical gardens, housing a massive zoological collection including a rare blue whale skeleton, thousands of insects, reptiles, and taxidermy specimens. Underrated by tourists, adored by locals.
🕗 1–2 hrs 💰 Small fee 📍 Kebun Raya
🦋 Combine this with the botanical garden visit — they're right next door. A great refuge when afternoon rain arrives suddenly.
Local Life
Jalan Surya Kencana
Bogor's vibrant Chinatown street, lined with street food stalls, traditional shops, and the aromas of ngohiang, soto kuning, and bubur ayam. This is where Bogor locals truly eat. A sensory feast day or night.
🕗 1–2 hrs 💰 Very cheap 📍 Bogor Timur
🍜 Go hungry. Start with a bowl of soto kuning, grab some ngohiang dimsum, and finish with a cup of es pala (nutmeg drink). The stalls get busiest from 10am–2pm.
Family
Kuntum Farmfield
A working agro-edu-tourism farm where you can feed animals, pick fruit, plant rice, and learn about traditional Sundanese farming. One of the best family attractions in Bogor — interactive, educational, and genuinely charming.
🕗 2–3 hrs 💰 ~IDR 50,000 📍 Bogor Tengah
🐄 Best with kids aged 4–12. Arrive in the morning for feeding sessions. The fresh milk from the farm cafe is genuinely excellent.
Culinary Guide

Eat & Drink

Bogor's food scene is a wonderful blend of Sundanese tradition, Javanese influence, and Chinese-Indonesian flavors. The rainy climate means locals love warming soups above all else.

Must-Try Dishes

  • 🍲
    Soto Bogor
    Bogor's signature yellowish beef soup, made with coconut milk and warming spices. The perfect antidote to an afternoon shower.
  • 🥗
    Asinan Bogor
    A refreshing pickled vegetable and fruit salad with a sweet-spicy-sour sauce. Unique to Bogor and utterly addictive.
  • 🍜
    Toge Goreng
    Fried bean sprouts with noodles, tofu, and sticky rice cake in a dark fermented soybean sauce. A true Bogor original.
  • 🥟
    Ngohiang
    Chinese-Sundanese fried meat rolls and dumplings — a Jalan Surya Kencana staple. Each family stall has their own secret recipe.
  • 🍞
    Roti Unyil
    Thumb-sized breads with creative fillings — chocolate, cheese, coconut, strawberry jam. Famous souvenir. Look for the queues on Jalan Pajajaran.
  • 🥧
    Pia Apple Pie
    Bogor's most beloved pastry shop — flaky apple pies that disappear fast. On Jalan Pangrango, expect a queue.

Coffee Shops Worth Finding

Bogor's rainy-day culture was practically built for lingering over coffee. The city's café scene ranges from hushed specialty roasters to lively hill-view hangouts.

☕

A city that drinks coffee seriously

Sitting at 300m altitude with near-daily rain, Bogor has cultivated one of West Java's most vibrant café cultures. Local baristas source beans from nearby Javan and Sumatran plantations, and the cool climate means a hot cup of kopi isn't just a drink — it's an event. Expect everything from no-frills warung kopi with kopi tubruk to polished specialty bars and view cafés that look out over volcanic peaks.

Before You Go

Travel Essentials

Everything a first-time visitor needs to know before arriving in the Rainy City.

Getting There

  • 🚆KRL Commuter Line — The easiest way. Trains from Jakarta Kota, Tanah Abang, or Manggarai to Bogor Station run every 10–20 minutes. Journey: ~1 hour. Fare: IDR 6,000. Air-conditioned and reliable.
  • 🚗By Car/Taxi — Via the Jagorawi Toll Road. Takes 1–1.5 hrs outside rush hour, or 3+ hrs during Jakarta peak times. Driving yourself or Grab Car are both viable options.
  • 🚌Bus (DAMRI) — Affordable buses from Kampung Rambutan terminal in Jakarta. Slower than the train but good if you're coming from South Jakarta.

Getting Around

  • 📱Grab & Gojek — The most convenient way to get around. Both apps work well in Bogor and are very affordable.
  • 🚐Angkot — Small minibuses that ply fixed routes around the city. Extremely cheap (IDR 3,000–5,000). Worth trying for the local experience, though crowded.
  • 🛺Becak — Three-wheeled cycle rickshaws are available near the train station and market areas. Good for short distances. Negotiate the fare first.
  • 🚶On Foot — The city center around Kebun Raya is very walkable. The botanical garden alone can occupy an entire day of gentle exploration.

Money & Practicalities

  • 💵Currency: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). ATMs are widely available, especially near Botani Square mall and Jalan Pajajaran.
  • 🍽️Food budget: IDR 50,000–150,000/day for street food; IDR 200,000–500,000 for restaurant meals.
  • 📶SIM Cards: Buy a Telkomsel or XL SIM at the train station or any Indomaret/Alfamart convenience store. Data is cheap and fast.
  • 🕌Friday prayer: Many shops and attractions reduce hours or close 11:30am–1:30pm on Fridays. Plan ahead.

Local Customs & Tips

  • ☂️Always carry an umbrella. Rain arrives with very little warning, usually in the afternoon. A small fold-up umbrella is your best travel companion here.
  • 🤝Bargain respectfully at markets — it's expected and part of the experience. Smile and be friendly; locals respond warmly.
  • 👗Dress modestly when visiting mosques, the Batutulis shrine, or religious sites. Covered shoulders and knees are appreciated.
  • 🍽️Eat with your right hand if eating without utensils — this is standard local etiquette across Indonesia.
  • 📸Ask before photographing people, especially at markets and places of worship. Most locals are happy to oblige with a smile.

☔ The Rain City Warning

Bogor receives some of the highest annual rainfall of any city in Indonesia — hence its nickname. Afternoon downpours are frequent and can be heavy. Plan outdoor activities for the morning, and have an indoor backup plan (museum, restaurant, café) ready for afternoons. Locals embrace the rain as part of Bogor's soul — so should you.
