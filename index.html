The Brewing Ritual

Follow these steps precisely. Your equipment has been accounted for.

1

Preheat Everything

Hand press devices lose heat fast. Run hot water through the empty chamber once to warm the metal. This prevents sour, under-extracted shots.

Target: 90-96°C water for dark roast Bajawa
2

Dose the Coffee

Since you're using pre-ground Bajawa, fill the basket to 8-10 grams depending on your handpress model. Level it off but don't compress yet.

Pro tip: Bajawa pre-ground tends to be slightly coarser than ideal for handpress. Fill on the higher end (10g) to slow extraction.
3

Tamp with Pressure

Use the back of a spoon or your thumb to press down firmly. You're aiming for ~15-20 lbs of pressure—firm but not bodyweight.

Too loose = watery shot Too tight = no flow
4

Lock and Load

Attach the portafilter/brew head tightly. Add 50-60ml hot water to the chamber. Screw on the pump mechanism securely.

5

The Press (Critical)

Pump slowly and steadily. Aim for 8-10 bars of pressure (firm resistance). First drops should appear in 3-5 seconds.

Bajawa timing: Total extraction should take 20-30 seconds. The dark chocolate notes extract late, so don't rush the last third.
6

Sweeten with Palm Sugar

While espresso is hot, add liquid palm sugar. Stir vigorously for 10 seconds to integrate.

7

Serve & Savor

Bajawa's low acidity and full body pair beautifully with palm sugar's caramel notes. Look for the lingering tobacco and floral finish.

8

Clean Immediately

Palm sugar residue gets sticky fast. Rinse all parts with hot water within 2 minutes of brewing.

Rescue Your Shot

Too Bitter / Harsh

  • → Lower water temp slightly (85-88°C)
  • → Reduce dose to 8g (pre-ground may be too fine)
  • → Add palm sugar before tasting to balance

Too Sour / Weak

  • → Preheat more aggressively (2 rinse cycles)
  • → Tamp harder—aim for 20+ lbs pressure
  • → Extend extraction to 30+ seconds if possible

No Crema / Thin

  • → Check coffee freshness (pre-ground stales in 2-3 weeks)
  • → Use finer grind if you switch beans later
  • → Ensure full pressure build-up before first pump

Palm Sugar Not Dissolving

  • → Add immediately while espresso is >80°C
  • → Stir with a spoon, not just swirling
  • → If crystallized, warm the bottle in water first
