The Iced Ritual

Brew hot, chill fast, layer beautifully.

1

Brew Double Strength

Use 10-12g Bajawa pre-ground with only 30-40ml water. You're making a concentrate that ice and milk will dilute.

Target: 20-25 second extraction for concentrate
2

Dissolve Palm Sugar First

While the espresso is hot, add palm sugar and stir vigorously for 15 seconds. It must dissolve completely before hitting the ice.

Why hot: Palm sugar won't dissolve well in cold liquid. This is your only chance.
3

Flash Chill (Critical)

Pour the hot sweetened concentrate over a full glass of ice (not just a few cubes). Stir for 10 seconds until the glass feels cold.

Don't let it sit hot Ice is your chiller
4

Add Cold Milk

Pour 120-150ml cold milk over the iced coffee. Whole milk gives body; oat milk adds a malty note that pairs with palm sugar.

5

Layer or Mix

For the gradient effect: pour milk slowly over the back of a spoon. For full integration: stir vigorously until uniform caramel color.

Pro move: The layered look lasts about 30 seconds before settling. Serve immediately if showing off.
6

Adjust & Taste

After the first sip, decide: too strong? Add milk. Too sweet? More ice. Too weak? You need more concentrate next time, not less ice.

7

Serve with a Straw

The layers taste different as you drink. A straw lets you control whether you get the sweet bottom, creamy middle, or coffee top.

8

Clean While Cold

Palm sugar hardens as it cools. Rinse your hand press and stirring spoon with warm water immediately.

Rescue Your Iced Coffee

Too Weak / Watery

  • → Brew concentrate stronger (less water, same coffee)
  • → Use more ice, not less—then less milk
  • → Check pre-ground freshness (stales faster exposed to humidity)

Palm Sugar Gritty / Undissolved

  • → Must dissolve in HOT coffee before any ice touches it
  • → If crystallized, warm the bottle in hot water first
  • → Use simple syrup as backup (1:1 palm sugar:water, heated)

Too Bitter When Cold

  • → Lower brew temp to 85-88°C (cold magnifies bitterness)
  • → Reduce tamp pressure slightly
  • → Add more milk or palm sugar to balance

Layers Mix Too Fast

  • → Pour milk over back of spoon onto ice, not directly into coffee
  • → Ensure coffee is fully chilled before adding milk
  • → Use a clear glass and serve immediately for photos
